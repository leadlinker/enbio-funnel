The Problem

Most Practices Buy the Wrong Autoclave. Here's Why.

Sterilization equipment is one of the biggest investments in any practice. But most buyers make their decision based on price alone, without understanding the differences that actually impact daily workflow, compliance, and long-term cost.

  • 1

    Class N, Class S, Class B: The Difference Matters

    Not all autoclaves are equal. Only Class B vacuum sterilizers handle every load type (wrapped, porous, hollow). Most practices do not know this until they fail an inspection.

  • 2

    Cycle Time Kills Productivity

    A 45-minute cycle means fewer instruments available during peak hours. The difference between 7 minutes and 45 minutes adds up to hours of lost productivity every week.

  • 3

    Hidden Costs Add Up Fast

    Plumbing installation, water consumption, maintenance contracts, and replacement parts. The sticker price is just the beginning. Total cost of ownership is what matters.

  • 4

    Compliance Requirements Keep Changing

    OSHA, CDC, and state boards update sterilization guidelines regularly. Your autoclave needs to meet current standards and have the documentation to prove it.
